One-carbon (1C) metabolism is the set of folate-dependent and folate-independent biochemical pathways that transfer single-carbon units, supporting nucleotide synthesis, methylation reactions, and — in bacteria — the production of formate.

Exercise depletes folate-dependent 1C precursors (folic acid, glycine, sarcosine, threonine, dimethyl glycine, histidine) in the gut microbiota while increasing downstream **formate** output, linking exercise-induced microbiota remodeling to antitumor CD8 T cell immunity.
