The Hippo pathway is a signaling axis in which ECM detachment normally activates Lats1/2 kinases to inactivate the transcriptional co-activators YAP and TAZ, driving anoikis.

In cancer, this axis is frequently deregulated so that YAP/TAZ remain active after detachment, blocking anoikis; experimentally, knocking down YAP/TAZ restores anoikis sensitivity in these cells.
