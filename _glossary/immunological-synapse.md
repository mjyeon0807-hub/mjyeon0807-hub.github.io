The immunological synapse is the organized interface — comprising a central supramolecular activation cluster (cSMAC) and a peripheral SMAC (pSMAC) — formed between an immune cell and its target or antigen-presenting cell during receptor engagement.

It is the site where trogocytosis occurs: ligand-receptor engagement at the synapse triggers signaling (Lck/Syk → PI3K → actin remodeling in T cells; Src-kinase/Ca2+/PKC in NK cells) that drives membrane transfer between the two cells.
