---
title: "Homotypic vs. Heterotypic Clustering"
term_id: clustering
category: tumor-microenvironment
related: [ctc, ctm]
sources: [kim-2026-ctc]
---

CTC clusters are groups of circulating tumor cells that travel together in the bloodstream and are markedly more metastatic than single CTCs. **Homotypic clusters** are formed of CTCs alone, stabilized by plakoglobin-dependent desmosomes and CD44 homophilic adhesion, while **heterotypic clusters** also include host cells such as platelets (via P-selectin/PSGL-1), neutrophils (via IL-6/IL-1β and ICAM-1), or cancer-associated fibroblasts (CAFs).

The Na⁺/K⁺-ATPase inhibitor digoxin can dissociate these clusters — in a Phase I trial (DICCT, NCT03928210), it reduced mean cluster size by 2.2 cells, though cardiotoxicity limits its clinical use.
