Nrf2 (nuclear factor erythroid 2-related factor 2) is a pleiotropic transcription factor that regulates redox homeostasis and metabolism.

In CD8 T cells, Nrf2 is required downstream of the microbiota metabolite **formate** for Tc1 effector differentiation: knocking out Nrf2 globally, blocking it pharmacologically (ML-385), or deleting it specifically in CD8 T cells all abolish formate's antitumor and Tc1-enhancing effects. Formate activates Nrf2 to increase pSTAT1 and IRF8, and the synthetic Nrf2 agonist TBHQ independently reproduces formate's antitumor effect.
