Necroptosis is a regulated, inflammatory form of programmed cell death that is mechanistically distinct from apoptosis: rather than depending on caspase activation, it is executed through the necrosome, a signaling complex built around RIP1/RIP3 kinases, ultimately leading to plasma membrane rupture. Because it is inflammatory, necroptotic death — unlike the comparatively immunologically silent apoptosis — can further shape the surrounding immune microenvironment.
