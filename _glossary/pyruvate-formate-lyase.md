Pyruvate formate lyase (pfl, EC 2.3.1.54) is a bacterial enzyme that converts pyruvate into formate and acetyl-CoA. Because it is the rate-limiting step of microbial formate production, the *pfl* gene is used as a marker of a microbiome's formate-producing capacity.

A pfl-knockout strain of commensal *E. coli* was used to isolate formate as the specific antitumor metabolite (rather than other bacterial components like LPS): wild-type (formate-producing) *E. coli* suppressed melanoma growth and boosted Tc1 immunity, but the pfl-deficient strain did not. In human cohorts, higher fecal *pfl* abundance associates with better response to PD-1 immune checkpoint therapy.
