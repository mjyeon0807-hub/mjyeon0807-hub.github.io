Fecal microbiota transplantation (FMT) is the transfer of donor fecal microbiota into antibiotic-pretreated recipient mice (or, in translational studies, from human donors into mice) to test whether a donor's microbiome phenotype causally drives a physiological effect.

FMT from exercised mouse donors reproduced exercise's antitumor effect in sedentary recipients, establishing the gut microbiota as necessary and sufficient for exercise-induced tumor suppression. In human translation, FMT from "Formate-HIGH" human stool donors conferred greater melanoma suppression and Tc1 immunity in mice than FMT from "Formate-LOW" donors.
