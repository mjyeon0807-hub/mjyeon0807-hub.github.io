Immune checkpoint inhibitors (ICIs), such as anti-PD-1 and anti-PD-L1 antibodies, are cancer immunotherapies that release brakes on T cell antitumor activity.

The gut-microbiota metabolite **formate** synergizes with anti-PD-L1 to restrain melanoma growth in mice, and in human melanoma patients treated with PD-1 checkpoint therapy, higher fecal *pfl* (formate-producing gene) abundance and higher serum formate both associate with better ICI response and longer progression-free survival — positioning formate as both a potentiator of and biomarker for checkpoint immunotherapy.
