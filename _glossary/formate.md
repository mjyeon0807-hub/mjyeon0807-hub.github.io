Formate is a short-chain, one-carbon organic acid and a key product of microbial one-carbon (1C) metabolism, produced by gut bacteria via the enzyme pyruvate formate lyase (pfl).

Exercise remodels the gut microbiota to increase cecal and serum formate, which is both necessary and sufficient to restrain melanoma growth and enhance the efficacy of immune checkpoint inhibitors (ICI) in mice. Formate acts by activating **Nrf2** in CD8 T cells, driving their differentiation into cytotoxic Tc1 effector cells. In humans, higher fecal abundance of the *pfl* gene and higher serum formate both associate with better outcomes on PD-1 checkpoint therapy in melanoma patients.
